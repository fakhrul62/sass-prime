UNIVERSAL DIGITAL DESIGN GUIDELINE

PURPOSE

Use this file as the default visual and interaction direction for modern
websites. The target is clean, minimal, editorial, technical, responsive, and
highly polished. Adapt the content and identity to each product without losing
the underlying system.


1. VISUAL CHARACTER

The site should feel:

- clean and minimal
- bold without becoming loud
- editorial but digital-first
- technical without looking like a generic dashboard
- spacious without wasting screen space
- animated without feeling restless
- premium without excessive blur, gradients, or shadows

Build identity from controlled typography, strong contrast, strict alignment,
thin dividers, deliberate color fields, quality imagery, one recognizable
interaction motif, and small technical labels.

Avoid generic templates, repeated rounded cards, excessive centered layouts,
glassmorphism everywhere, random gradient blobs, pills on every control,
unreadably oversized headings, and decorative animation without purpose.


2. TYPOGRAPHY

Always use clean, minimal fonts. Prefer modern grotesk, geometric sans, or
neo-grotesk families. Use no more than two proportional families. A mono font
is optional and should be limited to metadata, labels, and data.

Recommended systems:

System A - Bold editorial technology
  Display: Archivo Black
  Body/UI: DM Sans
  Labels: IBM Plex Mono

System B - Clean modern product
  Display: Space Grotesk
  Body/UI: Inter
  Labels: IBM Plex Mono

System C - Soft geometric minimalism
  Display: Urbanist
  Body/UI: DM Sans
  Labels: Roboto Mono

System D - Refined SaaS
  Display: Plus Jakarta Sans
  Body/UI: Inter
  Labels: IBM Plex Mono

System E - Single-family restraint
  Display, body, and UI: Inter, DM Sans, or Plus Jakarta Sans
  Labels: the same family in uppercase with increased tracking

Rules:

- Use variable fonts where practical.
- Load only required weights and character sets.
- Always provide system fallbacks.
- Avoid body weights below 400.
- Use 600 or 700 for emphasis.
- Never combine two expressive display fonts.
- Avoid decorative serif or script fonts unless explicitly requested.

Type rules:

- Keep hero statements concise, normally 6 to 11 words.
- Keep body copy between 45 and 75 characters per line.
- Use text-wrap: balance for short headings.
- Use text-wrap: pretty for paragraphs where supported.
- Never shrink important text merely to force one line.
- Never let text animation split individual words.
- Use uppercase for short labels, not paragraphs.
- Keep mobile body text at 15.5px or larger.
- Cap headings on ultrawide screens.


4. COLOR SYSTEM

Use one dark foundation, one light foundation, one primary accent, one
secondary accent, and one optional signal color. Foundations should occupy most
of the interface. Use bright accents for action, state, or section changes.

  Gold:      #E5B94F

Color rules:

- Use dark text on bright accent surfaces.
- Use near-white text on dark surfaces.
- Normal text needs at least 4.5:1 contrast.
- Large text and essential graphics need at least 3:1 contrast.
- Check translucent colors against their rendered background.
- Never use color as the only state indicator.
- Reserve the brightest color for primary actions or one major visual moment.
- Prefer confident flat color fields over unnecessary gradients.
- Avoid pure black and white when softer ink and paper feel more refined.

Suggested balance:

  65 to 75 percent foundation surface
  15 to 25 percent secondary surface
  5 to 10 percent accent and signal colors

Define primitive palette tokens, then map them to semantic roles:

  --surface-primary
  --surface-secondary
  --surface-elevated
  --text-primary
  --text-secondary
  --text-inverse
  --border-primary
  --border-soft
  --action-primary
  --focus-ring

Theme sections by changing semantic tokens on the section container. Child
components should inherit the active theme.


5. GRID AND SPACING

Mobile:
  6 columns
  16px outer margin
  10px to 12px gutter

Desktop:
  12 columns
  32px to 48px outer margin
  20px to 28px gutter

Wide desktop:
  keep 12 columns
  cap inner content around 1600px to 1760px
  allow backgrounds and selected media to remain full bleed

Rules:

- Align unrelated sections to shared column starts.
- Prefer asymmetric 5/7, 4/8, or 7/5 compositions.
- Use borders and whitespace as primary separators.
- Avoid placing every section inside a centered card.
- Maintain consistent outer gutters.
- Never allow edge-to-edge text on mobile.

Spacing:

  4px   micro adjustment
  8px   tight internal gap
  12px  compact control gap
  16px  default small gap
  24px  content gap
  32px  component separation
  48px  large component separation
  64px  small section rhythm
  96px  medium section rhythm
  128px large section rhythm
  160px to 240px major desktop section rhythm

Fluid sections:

  small:  clamp(3rem, 6vw, 6rem)
  medium: clamp(4.5rem, 9vw, 10rem)
  large:  clamp(6rem, 13vw, 15rem)

Use large gaps between ideas and smaller gaps inside an idea. Reduce section
padding before reducing text readability.


6. SHAPES AND SURFACES

- Use mostly square or lightly rounded surfaces.
- Use 0 to 8px radius for editorial panels.
- Use 10px to 16px radius for friendly product surfaces.
- Reserve pills for tags, compact filters, and status controls.
- Use circles for icon buttons, indicators, or one recurring motif.
- Use 1px low-contrast dividers.
- Use 2px borders only for intentionally graphic components.
- Keep soft shadows subtle and rare.
- A solid offset shadow may support one bold editorial object.

Choose one dominant shape system. Do not mix sharp panels, soft large-radius
cards, and glass pills without a clear reason.


7. PAGE COMPOSITION

Recommended rhythm:

1. compact header
2. strong hero statement
3. short context or proof strip
4. primary value or capability section
5. product or media demonstration
6. process, use cases, or principles
7. proof or metrics
8. focused conversion section
9. large typographic footer

Hero rules:

- Use one clear heading.
- Add one concise supporting paragraph.
- Use one primary and at most one secondary action.
- Include a useful visual rather than decorative filler.
- Keep navigation and hero content readable above the fold.
- Avoid a generic centered heading over a gradient blob.

Section rules:

- Give each viewport one dominant idea.
- Alternate density, scale, layout, and surface color.
- Use one dominant visual gesture per section.
- Keep explanatory copy close to the content it explains.
- Prefer rows, grids, and split layouts over repetitive card carousels.


8. MEDIA

Prefer editorial photography, product interface details, architectural crops,
abstract data graphics, and intentional monochrome or duotone treatments.

Avoid generic stock-office photography, random 3D blobs, low-resolution assets,
unrelated imagery, and generated images with visible defects.

Rules:

- Keep semantic HTML images and video as the reliable content layer.
- Use deliberate focal positioning.
- Decode images before reveal animations.
- Provide meaningful alt text.
- Keep hover zoom between 1.02 and 1.06.
- Never make essential information depend on canvas or WebGL.


9. MOTION SYSTEM

Motion must explain hierarchy, state, direction, or continuity. Use one main
motion language consistently.

Preferred:

- clipped vertical text reveals
- short fades with small transforms
- opposing layers for button hovers
- section-aware color transitions
- subtle image scale or displacement
- measured panel expansion
- small status animations
- pointer response on precise pointer devices

Avoid animating every element, large bouncing movement, elastic easing,
meaningless spinning, parallax everywhere, delayed reading, and scroll
hijacking.

Durations:

  instant feedback: 100ms to 180ms
  hover response:   180ms to 350ms
  small reveal:     350ms to 550ms
  text reveal:      600ms to 900ms
  panel transition: 500ms to 850ms
  media reveal:     800ms to 1200ms
  ambient settling: 1200ms to 2200ms

Easing:

  entrance: cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
  UI:       cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)
  exit:     cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1)

Rules:

- Exits should usually be faster than entrances.
- Direct manipulation must respond immediately.
- Prefer transforms and opacity.
- Avoid layout animation when transforms can achieve the result.
- Use stagger intervals between 25ms and 80ms.
- Do not stagger long lists one item at a time.


10. REVEALS

Default content reveal:

  initial:
    opacity: 0
    transform: translateY(24px to 48px)

  entered:
    opacity: 1
    transform: translateY(0)

  duration:
    650ms to 850ms

Use IntersectionObserver and reveal once. Trigger before the element reaches
the viewport center. Group related children and disconnect one-shot observers.

Major heading reveal:

  initial:
    opacity: 0
    translateY: 70% to 100%
    scale: 0.97
    clip-path closed below the line

  entered:
    opacity: 1
    translateY: 0
    scale: 1
    clip-path open

Use a 30ms to 60ms stagger between words or lines. Preserve whole words and the
semantic heading. Reserve split reveals for major statements.


11. INTERACTIONS

Primary buttons:

- minimum height 48px; preferably 52px to 60px
- clear border and strong contrast
- concise label
- optional directional icon
- visible focus state
- no width change on hover

Recommended button hover:

- normal label exits upward inside a clipped wrapper
- duplicate label enters from below
- normal arrow exits while an alternate arrow enters
- background changes through an overlay
- control may lift by no more than 2px to 4px

Text links should use an underline, arrow, or controlled offset. Keep arrow
movement between 3px and 8px.

Row hover:

- fill the background from one edge
- switch colors through semantic tokens
- move content 8px to 18px
- rotate or translate the arrow

Card hover:

- translate upward 4px to 10px
- scale media to 1.02 to 1.05
- increase border contrast or change the surface

Choose one primary response and one secondary detail.


12. NAVIGATION AND LOADING

The header should remain secondary to the page.

- Start transparent or low-opacity where appropriate.
- Gain a solid or blurred surface after scrolling.
- Adapt semantic colors to section themes.
- Preserve a clear current-page state.
- Keep one primary action.
- Use an accessible small-screen menu.

Mobile menus must open quickly, lock background scrolling, close on Escape and
route change, preserve keyboard focus, and use large readable links.

Every delayed route or action needs feedback:

- show a minimal route indicator only when loading is perceptible
- preserve button width during loading
- prevent duplicate form submission
- show success or error near the initiating control
- avoid full-screen loaders for content already available


13. POINTER EFFECTS

Pointer effects are optional progressive enhancement.

Allowed:

- one shared cursor label
- subtle magnetic controls
- low-resolution signal wake or particle field
- media tilt limited to approximately one degree
- pointer-driven mask or highlight

Disable on touch, coarse pointers, and reduced motion. Keep overlays
click-through, use one global pointer stream, stop rendering after movement
decays, and keep labels inside viewport edges.


14. FORMS AND MICRO-INTERACTIONS

Use small technical animation for live status, counters, filters, copied states,
form success, and menu indicators.

Character scramble is allowed only for short labels. Start it after mount,
preserve width, finish within 350ms to 700ms, and keep a stable accessible
label.

Forms:

- use visible labels, not placeholders alone
- keep fields at least 48px high
- show clear focus
- provide specific errors
- group related fields
- request only necessary information
- preserve values after recoverable errors
- use correct input types and autocomplete


15. RESPONSIVE COMPOSITION

Main behavioral breakpoint:
  approximately 960px to 1024px

Supporting checkpoints:
  360px, 600px, 768px, 1000px, 1280px, and 1440px

Rules:

- Treat mobile as a separate composition.
- Convert asymmetric desktop splits into a deliberate sequence.
- Remove hover-only dependencies.
- Keep essential content visible without interaction.
- Avoid horizontal overflow.
- Keep touch targets at least 44px by 44px.
- Test real long headings and content.


16. ACCESSIBILITY

Required:

- semantic HTML
- logical heading order
- visible keyboard focus
- keyboard-operable controls
- meaningful alternative text
- labels for icon-only actions
- sufficient contrast
- no essential state communicated by color alone
- readable content without canvas effects

For prefers-reduced-motion, remove parallax, cursor-following motion, large
transforms, and ambient loops. Reveal text immediately or with a short fade.
Preserve necessary state transitions.

Use a clear 2px to 3px focus ring with enough offset and contrast.


17. PERFORMANCE

- Prefer CSS for simple transitions.
- Use requestAnimationFrame for continuous effects.
- Share global scroll and pointer streams.
- Pause animation outside the viewport.
- Disconnect completed observers.
- Avoid layout reads and writes in the same loop.
- Use will-change only during active animation.
- Lazy-load below-the-fold media.
- Limit font files and weights.
- Use responsive image sizes.
- Do not ship WebGL when CSS is adequate.
- Keep the page usable before enhancement code runs.


18. REQUIRED TOKENS

Define before component styling:

Color:
  primitive palette, semantic surfaces, text, borders, actions, focus

Typography:
  display XL, display, heading 1 through 4, lead, body, small, label

Spacing:
  local scale, section scale, grid margin, grid gap

Shape:
  radius small, radius medium, radius full, border width

Motion:
  duration fast, UI, reveal, panel; entrance, UI, and exit eases

Layering:
  base, content, sticky, header, overlay, modal, cursor label


19. QUALITY CHECKLIST

- The first screen communicates one clear idea.
- Typography has deliberate scale and readable line lengths.
- Mobile headings do not overwhelm the viewport.
- One accent dominates at a time.
- Text and controls meet contrast requirements.
- Sections align to a consistent grid.
- Spacing follows a repeatable rhythm.
- The page does not depend on generic repeated cards.
- Hover states are purposeful and consistent.
- Keyboard focus is visible.
- Motion explains state or hierarchy.
- Reduced-motion mode remains polished.
- Touch devices do not receive fake hover behavior.
- Images are sharp, relevant, and correctly cropped.
- Forms show loading, success, and error states.
- Route changes provide feedback when needed.
- There is no horizontal overflow.
- Continuous animation pauses when inactive.


20. FINAL CREATIVE RULE

Do not make every site identical. Preserve the system, then choose one strong
identity device: a distinctive color transition, typographic motif, signal or
grid animation, editorial image treatment, custom icon language, section-aware
navigation element, or unique but usable content layout.

One memorable system executed consistently is stronger than many unrelated
effects. The result should be recognizable, restrained, responsive, and clear.
